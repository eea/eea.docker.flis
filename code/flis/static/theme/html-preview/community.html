How we work

Environmental policymakers face serious challenges

Policymakers working on environmental issues face serious and often competing challenges such as: limiting climate change and its impacts; halting the loss of biodiversity; and managing resource use. These challenges are complex, dynamic, long-term and often contested. Many causes and impacts are unknown. How such problems evolve over time and how they can be effectively influenced by policy is to a large extent uncertain. At the same time, because of the range of people and interests effected it can be difficult to reach a consensus on appropriate responses.

Strategic foresight can help deal with these challenges

Various futures analysis or foresight methods are available that may help policymakers to address complex issues through improved understanding of the challenges, and opportunities, they may present. For example, scenarios help explore the future development of specific challenges and policy alternatives to manage them. They can also help to streamline discussions, prioritise decisions and enhance engagement with and commitment from public, private and civic organisations. Trend analyses help understand past and present trends, like demographic, economic and technological developments and to explore the future directions they may take.
